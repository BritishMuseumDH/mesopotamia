Building Temples Higher

 

   Time/Ziggurats

 

Put these sentences in order to show how Mesopotamian temples got higher over time until somebody came up with the idea of ziggurats:

  • A way of making the temples even higher was by building huge stepped platforms (ziggurats).

  • A new temple was built on the remains of the old one.

  • Some ziggurats have the remains of older platforms and temples buried inside them.

  • Some platforms had more than one level.

  • Temples were built on mud brick platforms.

  • The remains of the old temple was buried in a bigger platform.

  • The remains of the temple formed a low platform of mud.

  • When an old temple needed rebuilding it was demolished.
