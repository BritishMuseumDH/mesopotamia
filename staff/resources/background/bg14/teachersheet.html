Calendar


 

 

The Sumerians, Babylonians and Assyrians used a lunar calendar with a year made up of 12 lunar months. As the average lunar month is 29.5 days, over 12 months this would produce a total of only 354 days as against a solar year of 365.25 days. To keep the calendar aligned to the seasons it was necessary for the Mesopotamians to add seven extra months in each period of 19 years.

The Mesopotamian year began in Nisannu. Months 6a and 12a are the additional months added into the year as required to adjust the calendar. This happened on the authority of the king after he received advice from experts in astronomy.
