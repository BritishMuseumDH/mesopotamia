A Front Page story


 
To explore Woolley's discoveries and tell the story to the public. To write a narrative of events using description and reporting.
Examples of newspaper reports
Teacher presentation / individual work

 

Discuss why newspapers are important.

  • How do newspapers get information?
  • Do you think it was Leonard Woolley or somebody else who wrote the newspaper accounts?
  • How would you make the account more exciting?
Ask the pupils to choose a year or event which they found interesting in the Tombs Story. Their job is to be a newspaper reporter and tell the general public. They should write a headline and a description of the object(s) found and why they are important. Drawings of site plans, objects or the archaeologists could be included.
