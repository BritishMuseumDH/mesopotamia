The siege engine

A siege engine is pushed up to the city walls. Two Assyrian archers stand at the top of the siege engine and shoot arrows. The enemy soldiers throw burning torches at the siege engine to try and set it on fire. However, the Assyrians pour water out of pipes at the front of the siege engine to put out any fire.

Enemy soldiers lower a chain to pull the battering ram at the front of the siege engine up and away from the city walls. Meanwhile, two Assyrian soldiers are pulling down on the chain with hooks.

Two archers crouch down next to the siege engine. They use the siege engine as protection from arrows while they are shooting at the enemy soldiers on top of the city walls.

Above the archers you can see a very small archer. He is probably meant to represent the Assyrian god Ashur helping the Assyrians in battle.
