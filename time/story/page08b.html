Woolley's workmen on the ziggurat at Ur

When Woolley and Koldewey were excavating in Mesopotamia they could afford to hire hundreds of workers who were not skilled archaeologists. This meant that large areas of a site could be excavated. For example, when the excavations at Babylon were closed in 1917 about half of the huge site had been uncovered.

Today archaeologists can rarely afford large numbers of diggers and have to rely on volunteers or explore only a small area of a site. This means they can work very carefully and record all the finds but does mean that a broad picture is not possible.


Archaeologists and workmen on a recent excavation in north Mesopotamia

Most of the archaeologists are specialists. For example, they can translate cuneiform, or are experts in bone or pottery or plant remains.
