When excavation began in the nineteenth century not enough was known about Mesopotamian history to work out a good chronology. People tried to use the Bible and Greek and Roman texts to work out when things might have happened.

Archaeologists started to use changes in the designs of pottery and other objects to work out which sites were older or younger. Ancient writing also helped because the shape of cuneiform signs changed through time. Some cuneiform tablets also included lists of events or kings which said how long each had reigned.

Today, scientific techniques like Carbon 14, thermoluminescence, and tree-ring dating give much more precise dating.
