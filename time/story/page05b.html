Layard loaded many of the sculptures he had discovered onto rafts which were floated down the River Tigris.

They were transferred to sailing ships and brought to Europe where many were displayed at the British Museum.

People became excited by these monuments which showed scenes of Assyrian warfare, religious ceremonies and royal building projects. The sculptures were so popular that people started making objects (such as bookends or flower pots) decorated in an 'Assyrian style'.
