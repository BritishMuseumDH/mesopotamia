The mouth of the river

The Tigris and the Euphrates become one river which then flows south to the Gulf.

The point where a river reaches the sea is called the 'mouth' of the river. Because rivers carry soil and pebbles with them as they cross the land and deposit them along the way, the mouths of the rivers, or even the whole coastline, may change over time.

Some scholars think that the coastline of the Gulf may have been very different in ancient times.
