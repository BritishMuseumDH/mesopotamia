The Sumerians believed that the world was made up of the rivers Tigris and Euphrates (they called them Idiglat and Purattu) with mountains beyond.

The sun and moon gods and the goddess Inanna (planet Venus) moved from the mountains across the sky. When people died their spirits went to the underworld through an entrance in the kur (mountains).

Later the people of Mesopotamia came to see their world slightly differently - they imagined it divided into heaven and air, earth where humans lived, underground ocean of fresh water and the kur (underworld).
